Constitutional Enforcement Checklist

"Warren Buffett identified five systemic loops that keep communities trapped. This checklist is your architectural counter to each one."

HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

For each of the five systemic traps, you'll find the diagnosis, the MAAT enforcement protocol, and three actionable steps. Complete all 15 actions to fully exit the loop. Check each box as you complete it.

Higher costs for the poor — payday loans, check-cashing fees, rent-to-own markups — silently drain wealth from those who can least afford it.

MAAT ENFORCEMENT — THE BASKET

Audit your monthly expenses for hidden 'poverty premiums' (late fees, check-cashing, high-interest debt).

Identify one institution (credit union, co-op, mutual aid) you can build or join to recycle wealth internally.

Replace at least one extractive financial relationship within 90 days.

The system is designed to make you seek immediate pleasure — impulse purchases, status symbols, dopamine-driven consumption — instead of building lasting value.

MAAT ENFORCEMENT — RITE OF PASSAGE

Implement a 72-hour rule: no purchase over $50 without a 3-day waiting period.

Create a 'Permanence List' — things you own that will still matter in 10 years.

Train one young person in your circle to distinguish spectacle from substance.

The Linear Income Trap

Trading finite time for capped income. If you stop working, money stops. You haven't built a business — you've built a treadmill.

MAAT ENFORCEMENT — THE CASHFLOW PILLAR

Identify your current income ceiling — what's the maximum you can earn with your time?

Research one 'boring business' model (laundromat, vending, storage) that generates income while you sleep.

Draft a 12-month plan to add at least one non-linear income stream.

The limiting beliefs of your peers pull you back into the trap. Your environment sets the ceiling for your ambition.

MAAT ENFORCEMENT — THE STORY

List the 5 people you spend the most time with. Honestly assess: are they building or consuming?

Study one historical example of your ancestors building generational institutions.

Replace one hour of entertainment per day with mentorship content or legacy planning.

Lack of growing assets. Without compounding wealth, every generation starts from zero — an endless reset loop.

MAAT ENFORCEMENT — THE NEVER-SELL DOCTRINE

Identify every asset you currently own that appreciates (real estate, investments, intellectual property).

Consecrate at least one asset as 'never-sell' — wealth that feeds seven generations.

Open or fund one account/asset specifically for the next generation, not for your personal use.
